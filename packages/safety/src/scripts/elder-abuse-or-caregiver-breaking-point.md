# Elder abuse and caregiver breaking point

## Direct answer

What you just shared takes real honesty, and it tells me how exhausted you are. This happens more often than caregivers talk about, and it’s a sign that you need support right now — not that you’re a bad person.

## Then

If anyone is in immediate physical danger, call 911. For ongoing risk or severe harm, Adult Protective Services is a place to call for *help* — for safety planning, services, and next steps. We can also point you toward respite, mental health follow-up, and the Alzheimer’s Association for practical support.

## Mandatory disclosure

We don’t promise confidentiality we can’t keep. Depending on your state, some conversations about abuse may be reportable. This is a place to get help — it’s not a place to report yourself. If you need a calm next step, start with a helpline; they can help you think through what to say and what the limits are in your state.

## What we do not do

- We do not shame you for reaching out.
- We do not give legal advice; we can name the pattern and where to call.
- We do not tell you to “tough it out” at home if safety is at risk.
